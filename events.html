Pride Week (JUNE 2020)

June , 2020

Pride week was a week long cultural extravaganza organized by Enactus Thapar to mark the pride month, organized from 18th to 26th June. It marked a strong effort from us to open the conversation upon the still taboo topic of the LGBTQ+ community, as it featured talks, panel discussions from prominent members of the LGBTQ+ community with the support of NGOs and other Enactus from North India.

Day 1- Q/A session with Mr. Anish Gawade

On the first day of our pride week, we welcomed one of the co-curators of the pink list, Mr. Anish Gawade for an interactive Q/A session hosted by Media and content Mentor Ms. Ananya Tiwari, as the two interacted in a fun filled session as Mr. Anish answered many of the questions asked by students through our Instagram handle.

Day 2- Panel discussion on Pride and India and Ally ship initiative

After the resounding success of our first day, we followed it up with a Panel discussion, as the participants discussed about the rise of the pride moment in India and how it has been shaping in the recent past. Many aspects like the government support, policy changes and society overview where discussed. Following this, we launched a social media campaign to reach out to students to get their view on what the LGBTQ+ community stands for them and what it means as students raised their voices for the same, allowing to us create an atmosphere of open conversation

Day 3 and Day 4

On the third and Fourth day of our event, we took a closer look at the various NGOs who are working to for the rights of the LGBTQ+ community and the various initiatives taken by them to ensure the upliftment of the community.

Day 5- Live Instagram session with Sade Gilberti

On the fifth day of our colosal event, we welcomed the South African TV actress, activist and content creator, Ms. Sade Gilberti for a live instagram session on our handle as she discussed about her life, her experiences, the difficulties that she face, the confusion, all aspects in thorough details that allowed viewers to gain a better insight into the community.

Day 6- Panel Discussion with Sade Gilberti

After a thrilling session on the fifth day, we again welcomed Ms. Gilberti to be a part of our Panel Discussion on the penultimate day of our event on the topic of Social Entrepreneurship for pride, as a number of students from across Enactus campuses in North India joined us as a healthy discussion was help on the topic with extreme enthusiasm from the participants making for an interactive session with Sade Gilberti.

Day 7- Pledge campaign

On the final day of our pride week, we organized a pledge campaign to raise awareness and end the taboo surrounding the LGBTQ+ community and their members, making path for a brighter future in this direction.

Métier: The Case Study Competition( JUNE 2020)

June , 2020

As corona swept the nation like a storm and students were forced to return back to their homes, Enactus Thapar decided to organize a Case study competition to help students locked up in homes tackle with the new realities of self-isolation. The competition received an overwhelming response with 550+ entries for the first round itself, where students had the choice to either participate solo or be in a team of 2 members.

About the Competition

The competition was divided into 3 rounds, as teams were judged on different skill sets. The first round was an online quiz which was organized on D2C platform which tested participants on their general knowledge of the entrepreneurship world. This was followed by the release of case studies based on which participants prepared a presentation. They then presented their ideas on a zoom meeting. They were judged by our judges Mr. Krishnakumar Devnally, the founder and director of mPower solutions and Mr. Nitish Mehta. They judged them based on their presentation, ideation and innovation skills. After the conclusion of this exhausting competition, the pole position was taken by SNUtion followed by Dunder Mifflin and Enactus IPCW.

Deepotsav ’19 (Enactus’ Annual Diwali Event)

October, 2019

The Annual Diwali event, Deepotsav ’19, generated funds through selling Diyas. It featured an Open-Mic to attract the crowds. The Diyas and most material was brought from local roadside sellers in Patiala. All the funds received were given back to the local selling community of Patiala. The event successfully created short-run employment opportunities for local sellers and raised awareness about the deteriorating and dependent state of the Local Sellers. Reused Cardboard, Thermocol and other leftovers from previous events were used, to cut on unnecessary wastage.
