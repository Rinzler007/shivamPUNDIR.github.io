About us

We are a non-profit organisation dedicated to improving the world through entrepreneurial action. We develop sustainable social enterprises to help alleviate some of the biggest challenges that society is facing today.

We Are Enactus Thapar

Enactus Thapar is a group of talented and motivated students from Thapar University striving to make a difference in their community and improving the quality of life through entrepreneurial action.
This organisation is driven exclusively by students representing all faculties within Thapar University, and we are proud to showcase our university not only to our community partners but to students from all across India during Regional and National Competitions.

  • Enactus Thapar, associated with the students of Thapar University, is a branch of Enactus India
  • Enactus Thapar has a membership of over 70 Thapar students who work to provide relief to local and international social issues through developing new businesses.
  • Enactus India is comprised of over 130 teams in India. Each team is associated with a university - we are Enactus Thapar!
  • We are currently working on a number of social and commercial businesses. These social enterprises give students the chance to gain real life experience working as a part of a business.
  • Students get the opportunity to develop both their professional and personal skills, whilst putting their time towards driving real local and international change.
  • We envision a brighter future where we advance social innovation through our impactful projects’, our empowering events, and our engagement with our community partners.

What We Do

We believe investing in students who take entrepreneurial action for others creates a better world for us all.

Seeing Opportunity

Our teams identify market opportunities in both local and international communities and undertake an in-depth market analysis to identify the needs and wants of the market. We do this to understand the key barriers and formulate ways to improve the livelihood of those communities.

Taking Action

After identifying the market opportunities, we create project groups of concurring students who create and implement enterprise focused on improving the economic, social and environmental conditions and transforming the lives of the communities.

Enabling progress

We enable progress by implementing projects that focus on long term sustainability of the community. For instance, one of our project focus on recruiting unemployed and homeless people to improve and equip them with transferable skills to help them back into the world of work.

Enactus

“To enable progress through entrepreneurial action.”
Enactus is an international not-for-profit organisation dedicated to inspiring students to improve the world through entrepreneurial action.
Businesses provide sponsorship and guidance to enable the student teams to undertake educational projects which empower people, by applying business and economic concepts and an entrepreneurial approach to improve their quality of life and standard of living.

National Competitions

When we compete to improve lives, We All Win.
#WeAllWin

National competitions are a showcase of how Enactus students are transforming lives and enabling progress through entrepreneurial action.

Enactus teams compete across 37 countries where the quality and impact of their projects will be evaluated by business leaders serving as judges. These dynamic and inspiring events encourage creativity and reward results, ultimately leading to stronger programs and more lives improved. From each national competition one team is selected to represent their country at Enactus World Cup. The 2019 World Cup will be hosted in San Jose, California, USA.

Teams from universities around the world present their projects to business and academic judges and are judged according to the world wide criterion which asks:

Which Enactus team most effectively used entrepreneurial action to empower people to improve their livelihoods in an economically, socially and environmentally sustainable way?
