Mushroom Day - Project Mushroomed Celebrations

February 11, 2018

Enactus Thapar executed Project Mushroomed in Village Miranpur near Patiala. The project involved a simple process of mixing 50% of wheat stubble with 50% of rice stubble and composting it under controlled circumstances to grow MUSHROOMS.
After being successful in growing Mushrooms ourselves we decided to organize an event encouraging other farmers to adopt this technique and help reducing air pollution levels and most importantly help making them some extra cash with the waste that they apparently burn to get rid off.

With the help and worthy guidance of Dr. Jaswinder Singh (Director KVK) we were able to successfully execute a speech offered by him to the farmers to motivate them against Stubble Burning. The event was also witnessed the members of the Young Farmer Association as the chief guests. We also set up a treat for the presented Farmers and our chief guests comprising of “MUSHROOM TIKKA” a traditional Indian Cuisine everyone loves to show them the proof of the successful execution of our project and proving them that burning rice stubble can be a huge loss from them.

Then our Team along with all the farmers visited the site where our Mushrooms were growing. The site is owned by Mr. Sukhdev Singh who helped us in our endeavour. It was a great learning opportunity for whole ENACTUS team and the farmers also learned a new way of making profit from a thing which has no value for them.

Further to spread the idea of our project we celebrated “Mushroom Day” in our respectivehostels of our university, where students were served with mushroom dishes in lunch and the team members present their as representatives talked about all the work involved in serving them the delicious mushrooms.
