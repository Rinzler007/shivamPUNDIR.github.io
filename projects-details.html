Project Naritva

2020

Project Naritva is an endeavor by Enactus TIET to raise awareness in rural and urban communities about the significance of using Biodegradable Sanitation Pads, with the development and distribution of the same.
One of the flagship projects of Enactus Thapar, and the most influential, is Project Naritva. Under the umbrella of this project, we, in collaboration with various national and International Girlup Organizations, Institutes, and other Companionships cater to the installation of contactless, IoT enabled, biodegradable pad dispensers in various Government and private, schools and colleges; along with an endowment of the same to rural and suburban menstruators. Along with this, we also administer and moderate various panel discussions and talk shows to spread awareness about the same. We are currently acquiring pads as per our specifications from Sakhi and Anandi pads' manufacturers, which even meet the standards set up by Green the Red and QCI organizations. Post lockdown, we are looking forward to setting up our manufacturing unit in the Thapar campus itself, from where we will be further administering all the pads.

Project Paddy Waste To Income

2020

Why do you hate winters? We hate it because we live in Delhi. The burning of paddy waste which leads to complete black polluted air has alternatives. The project Paddy Waste to Income aims the same. Since the stubble is produced in large amount, it's management is a real challenge. Working towards the same, Enactus Thapar is in collaboration with Vijay Laxmi Foundation which has over 100 NGOs working under it. The project works by targeting various industries who could buy stubble and could convert it into various useful products possible. Since the products manufactured from stubble has a better quality and are more affordable, they have a huge market. The Project Paddy Waste to income targets to connect the market with farmers, who could find burning stubble as the only option to deal with it. Considering the need for the project, Enactus Fleming, Canada is working in collaboration with us and has won National Idea award in Canada under the umbrella of same project. We are looking forward to connect more people in the chain with us.

Project Entracer

2020

With the COVID-19 situation posing greater risks than ever, it has become even more essential for any organization to curb any potential risks, and track the health of its employees/members. Hence the very need for sanitization and data accumulation of people at a higher speed at such spaces is mandatory to avoid and minimize exposure and contagion. The Entracer Contact Tracing System has thus developed a two-fold mechanism to reduce and detect Coronavirus risks for closed spaces/campuses. The System utilizes two instruments to achieve this:
1. Entracer Sanitization and Data Accumulation Device (ESDAD) - which accumulates user data such as thermal temperature data and oxygen saturation level.
2. Entracer Contact Tracing App (ECTA) - This utilizes the local data provided by ESDAD to detect and predict COVID risks.
Let’s take a look at the detailed briefing of both the instruments to understand how it works.
● Entracer Sanitization and Data Accumulation Device (ESDAD)
ESDAD is a proprietary device built by Team Entracer to locally collect data and automatically sanitize visitors entering through a gateway. The Data that is collected is as follows:
1. Roll No. (In future Aadhar ID) through a bar-code scanner.
2. Thermal Temperature through a temperature-sensing laser.
3. Oxygen-saturation level through an oximeter.
The following data is then relayed to the common server of Entracer, computed, and calculated for risk detection. On detection of risk, the machine denies entry to the visitor and flags the ID over the app, categorizing it as “High-Risk”.
● Entracer Contact Tracing App (ECTA)
ECTA is a proprietary application built over a cross-platform language (flutter) to detect contact traces based on data accumulated by ESDAD. It is soon going to be implemented in Thapar Campus.

Project Grameen

2020

Project Grameen is based around the principle of Precision Agriculture. The solution employs predictive models and machine learning algorithms to ensure that the vegetation receives exactly what it needs for optimum health and productivity.

The aim of our offering is to ensure profitability, sustainability and protection of the land.

This service will make the process of farming easier and more productive using the technology available to us in the correct way.

The first phase of our project includes a buggy and a drone. The drone will be used to make a thermal map of the whole field, collecting moisture data to give us optimum spots for planting. Once the precision points have been identified, the buggy will choose 5 optimum spots for soil analysis. The body includes a hydraulic drill fitted with sensors like, but not limited to, Water Content Sensor, Airflow Sensor, NPK Sensor, and a Pressure Sensor, which will take samples of soil, analyze them, and send the data to the server. All the data will be analyzed using predictive ML models. In case of multi-crop farming, the app would connote the best farming patterns to maximize crop yield. It will also apprise the user to treat specific precision spots with fertilizers to prune away the OTT use of fertilizers. Once the crop has been planted, the drone can be used for growth tracking and the buggy would be able to automate the irrigation process.

Project Khadyan

2020

Project Khadyan is an initiative aimed at providing non-conventional method for decomposing all kinds of organic waste produced at Student Hostels and Campus Residencies, using Trench Composting Bins and microbes by implementing "Bokashi method of composting " the compost obtain will be sent to traditional compost heap for further breakdown. Bokashi method of composting employs specific microbes which after fermentation makes “Bokashi Bran”, the key ingredient for Bokashi Composting. The composting requires only about 10 days to convert organic materials into useable material, and the nutrient value of the material is among the highest of any method of composting. The liquid drained off from the compost tank known as "Bokashi Tea" and is highly acidic and can be used as drain unblocker or diluted to form a fertilizer for domestic plants. All the products obtained in this short and simple process are having exceptional nutrient values and are ready to be sent to the fields and markets.
